One of my main missions I always strove for during my time at University is helping women in Computer Science feel empowered and that they have a sense of community within the male-dominated field. There have been instances where I was the only female in my class periods, making me feel a bit of an outsider. By taking the recuitment chair position for University of Minnesota's ACM-W chapter, I felt I was able connect and strengthen the female community in my school's CS student body. Learning to program is extremely rewarding but also very difficult, so by providing career / academic oppurtunities and a sense of belonging to these women does wonders in keeping them in this field.

In terms of my own career, I started TA'ing in a coding camp for high & middle school aged girls. The camp taught basic html / css / javascript to these girls and was intended to inspire them to pursue Computer Science by providing positive exposure to coding early on. After that, in Summer 2018 I was fortunate enough to spend a summer assisting in designing a study for Dr. Lina Battestilli at North Carolina State University; the study looked to improve CS education for non-CS majoring engineers. Specifically, we looked at the questions of autonomous grading systems to see if we could improve the way coding excercises were asked.

The last major experience I had was finally in industry; Summer 2019 I flew out to Manhattan to work as a software engineer intern at American Express. There, I learned a lot about front-end development, as my team worked on a webapp using JavaScript's React-Redux and Bootstrap libraries. It was the first real exposure I had with github, JavaScript, and front-end development in general. It was a hard learning curve but came with it was a strong sense of fulfillment! Now, I get the treat to train and learn with Google throught their Software Product Sprint and will soon be spending another two months virtually interning for American Express again.
